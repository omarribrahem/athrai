The primary objective of accounting: To provide useful financial information about a specific organization to interested users for making informed decisions, through an information system that identifies, records, and communicates economic events related to the entity.

ğŸ“š Core Concepts (80% Focus)

1) Three Accounting Activities

  • Identification: Distinguishing important economic events
  • Recording: Systematically recording, classifying, and summarizing events
  • Communication: Communicating information through financial reports to users

Practical Example:

In a coffee shop, purchasing inventory worth 500 is considered an economic event recorded as inventory against cash, while a non-financial conversation is not recorded.

2) Basic Accounting Equation

\[ \text{Assets} = \text{Liabilities} + \text{Owner's Equity} \]

Assets are financed either by debts (liabilities) or owners' investments (equity), and the equation must remain balanced after every transaction.

Practical Example:

Depositing 50,000 as capital increases both assets and equity by the same amount, and purchasing equipment for 10,000 cash changes asset composition without breaking the balance.

3) Five Components of Accounting Definition

  1. Economic Entity: Separating business transactions from owners
  2. Business Transactions: Economic events measurable in monetary terms
  3. Accounting Activities: Identification, recording, and communication
  4. Information System: Inputs, processing, and outputs
  5. Interested Users: Internal and external
